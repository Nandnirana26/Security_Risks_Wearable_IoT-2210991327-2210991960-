## 📄 Abstract

Wearable IoT devices such as smartwatches, fitness bands, and medical sensor monitors are increasingly being adopted in healthcare and personal lifestyle management. These devices continuously collect, process, and transmit sensitive user data — including biometric, physiological, and location information — making them attractive targets for cyberattacks.

This research paper evaluates the security risks associated with wearable IoT devices, identifies prevalent vulnerabilities and attack vectors (eavesdropping, device spoofing, malware injection, DoS, MITM, side-channel attacks, and firmware exploitation), and examines the effectiveness of existing countermeasures including lightweight cryptographic algorithms (PRESENT, SIMON, SPECK, Ascon), Elliptic Curve Cryptography (ECC), multi-factor authentication, and secure communication protocols.

**Keywords:** _Wearable IoT, Cybersecurity, Data Privacy, Lightweight Cryptography, IoT Vulnerabilities_

## 🔬 Research Methodology

- **Research Design:** Qualitative and analytical approach based on systematic review and synthesis of academic literature.
- **Data Sources:** IEEE Xplore, ACM Digital Library, Springer, ScienceDirect, Google Scholar, NIST, and SonicWall technical reports.
- **Scope:** Peer-reviewed studies published between 2013–2025 focusing on wearable IoT security, privacy, and vulnerability analysis.

---

## 📊 Key Findings

1. **Data interception** via BLE vulnerabilities remains one of the most prevalent and accessible threats.
2. **Weak authentication** (default credentials, lack of MFA) continues to enable unauthorized access.
3. **Lightweight cryptography** (PRESENT, SIMON, SPECK, Ascon) provides effective security within resource constraints.
4. **Firmware security** remains significantly neglected across the industry.
5. **Multi-layered defense** combining encryption, strong authentication, secure protocols, and anomaly detection is essential.
6. IoT malware attacks increased by **107% in 2024**, and average data breach cost reached **$4.88 million**.
